Heat map

A heat map is a visual representation of data that uses colour to show the values of two or more variables.

On this page

  • When to use
  • What to avoid
  • Design and code
  • Content and design guidelines
  • Accessibility guidelines

When to use

  • To show the relationship between two or more variables
  • To show the distribution of data over two or more dimensions
  • To identify trends or patterns in the data

Examples

  • A heat map showing the distribution of a certain risk factor for a disease, such as obesity or smoking
  • A heat map showing the distribution of antimicrobial consumption by province

What to avoid

  • Don’t use heat maps to show data that does not have a clear numeric value
  • Don’t use heat maps to show data with different units or different scales

    Content and design guidelines

    • Use a sequential or diverging colour palette (see Colours)
    • Include a legend for the colour palette
    • Considering including the value in each cell
    • Follow the general design guidelines

    Accessibility guidelines

    • Provide a data table with the graph
    • If the heat map tells a clear story, include that story in the alt text - for example, if the heat map shows a clear relationship or distribution of data, spell it out in the alt text
    • Follow the general accessibility guidelines
